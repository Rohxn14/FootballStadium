Iconic Football Stadiums

Explore some of the most iconic football stadiums from around the world.

Santiago Bernabeu

The Santiago Bernabéu Stadium is a famous football (soccer) stadium located in Madrid, Spain.
It is the home stadium of one of the most successful and well-known football clubs in the world, Real Madrid Club de Fútbol.

Home Team: Real Madrid C F

Camp Nou

The Camp Nou is another iconic football (soccer) stadium, located in Barcelona, Spain.
It is the home stadium of FC Barcelona, one of the most successful and popular football clubs in the world.

Home Team: FC Barcelona

Wembley

Wembley Stadium is a renowned sports and entertainment venue in London, UK, with a seating capacity of around 90,000.
It is famous for hosting historic football matches, including the 1966 FIFA World Cup Final.

Home Team: Tottenham Hotspur

Sri Kanteerava Stadium

The Kanteerava Stadium is a multi-purpose sports facility in Bangalore, India, named after Sri Kanteerava Narasimharaja Wadiyar.
Inaugurated in 1997, it hosts various sports events including athletics, football, and hockey, with a seating capacity of around 26,000.
Additionally, the stadium is utilized for cultural and entertainment events in the city.

Home Team: Bengaluru FC

Allianz Arena

The Allianz Arena, located in Munich, Germany, is a world-renowned football stadium.
Home to FC Bayern Munich and TSV 1860 Munich, its distinctive illuminated façade changes colors to match the home team's kit.

Home Team: Bayern Munich

Old Trafford

Old Trafford, located in Greater Manchester, England, is the iconic home stadium of Manchester United Football Club.
With a seating capacity of approximately 74,140 spectators, it stands as one of the UK's largest football venues.
Opening its doors in 1910, Old Trafford has hosted countless historic football matches and is celebrated as one of the sport's most iconic arenas.

Home Team: Manchester United
